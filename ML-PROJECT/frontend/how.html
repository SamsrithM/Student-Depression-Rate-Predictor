How it works

Overview

The predictor uses Logistic Regression on a set of college-related features: course failures, absences, going-out frequency, study time, health, and GPA. The alcohol habit is mapped to a risk signal internally. We evaluated additional models to compare performance.

Features and effects

  • Course failures: More failures increase risk.
  • Absences: Higher absences correlate with risk.
  • Going out: Very low social activity can increase risk.
  • Study time: Consistent study time generally lowers risk.
  • Health: Lower self-reported health increases risk.
  • GPA: Very low GPA increases risk.
  • Alcohol habit: Mapped to an alcohol signal; habit increases risk.
  • Family hours: More time with family generally supports emotional wellbeing.
  • Activity hours: Regular physical activities and hobbies help reduce stress and improve mood.
  • Sex/Age: Collected for context; not used in the current model.
